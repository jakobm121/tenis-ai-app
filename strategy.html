Bankroll management is one of the most important skills in sports betting. Proper money management prevents rapid losses and increases long-term profitability.

1. What is a Bankroll?

Your bankroll is the total amount of money you have set aside specifically for sports betting. It‚Äôs crucial that your bankroll is separate from your everyday finances, so betting does not put your personal budget at risk.

2. Key Principles of Bankroll Management

a) Determine Your Bet Size

A common method is the fixed percentage of your bankroll. Recommended bet size is usually 1‚Äì5% of your bankroll per wager, depending on risk and confidence.

b) Don‚Äôt Risk Too Much at Once

Large bets can quickly deplete your bankroll if things go wrong. It‚Äôs important to survive losing streaks without going into negative territory.

c) Adjust Bets Based on Performance

If your bankroll grows, you can proportionally increase your stakes. If you‚Äôre losing, reduce your stakes to protect your remaining bankroll.

3. Common Betting Strategies

  • Flat Betting ‚Äì every bet is the same percentage of your bankroll (usually 1‚Äì5%). The safest approach.
  • Percentage Betting ‚Äì bet size changes as your bankroll grows or shrinks.
  • Kelly Criterion ‚Äì a more advanced method that calculates the optimal percentage of your bankroll based on odds and probability of winning.

4. Keep Records

Track every bet, including odds, stake, outcome, and profit/loss. This helps you analyze performance and improve your strategy.

5. Psychological Aspect

Bankroll management helps control emotions and prevents impulsive betting. Without it, a small losing streak can quickly become disastrous.

6. Summary

  • Only bet with money you can afford to lose.
  • Use a fixed or adjustable percentage of your bankroll.
  • Adjust your bets according to results and long-term strategy.
  • Keep records and maintain discipline.

The Fibonacci system is a progressive betting strategy based on the famous Fibonacci sequence (1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13 ‚Ä¶), where each number is the sum of the two preceding ones. In betting, it is often used to recover losses and secure small profits after a win.

How It Works

  • Start with a base stake (e.g., 1 unit).
  • After every loss, move to the next number in the Fibonacci sequence.
  • After a win, move back two steps in the sequence.
  • The goal: one win should recover all previous losses and deliver a small profit.

Example

Let‚Äôs assume your base stake is 1 unit and you‚Äôre betting at odds of 2.0:

  • Bet 1 ‚Üí Lose
  • Bet 1 ‚Üí Lose
  • Bet 2 ‚Üí Lose
  • Bet 3 ‚Üí Win (return = 6 units)

Total staked = 1 + 1 + 2 + 3 = 7 units. Total return = 6 units. Net result = -1 unit. On the next win, you move back two steps and usually secure a profit.

Advantages

  • Less aggressive than Martingale ‚Äì stakes grow more slowly.
  • One win can cover previous losses.
  • Useful for markets with higher odds, such as football draws.

Disadvantages

  • Long losing streaks can still lead to very large bets.
  • Requires a large bankroll to survive variance.
  • Bookmakers have maximum bet limits that can break the sequence.

Best Use Case

The Fibonacci strategy is most commonly applied to draw betting in football, where odds typically range between 3.0‚Äì3.5. This makes it easier to recover losses and lock in profits.

Martingale is probably the most well-known betting system, and we have also had very positive experiences with it. It is a simple strategy: after each loss, you double your stake, and after a win, you return to the base amount. This allows you to take advantage of winning streaks and quickly recover losses. Important: Be careful in selecting bets, as a losing streak can lead to very high stakes and increased risk.

How It Works

  • Start with a base stake (e.g., 10 units).
